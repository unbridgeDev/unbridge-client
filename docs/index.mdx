# Unbridge

**The private multisig on Solana.** A vault your team controls together, where the chain
shows one ordinary wallet: no member list, no threshold, no visible balance, and no link
from one payment to the next.

> Program: `6ESjwd4u6qW8SP9PtNwNus1hBJTxKViWra91C36RRALu` (Solana mainnet)

---

## The problem

A normal multisig is transparent. Anyone reading the chain sees the signer set, the
threshold, the balance, and every payment in and out. For a team treasury that is a map
of who is involved, how much they hold, and who they pay. The security model leaks the
org chart.

Hiding it behind a single wallet is worse: now one key moves the money, and the "team"
is a social promise, not a rule.

## The idea

Keep the shared control of a multisig. Remove everything the chain can read off it.

- Funds sit in a **shielded pool**. Each deposit becomes a Poseidon commitment that hides the amount, the owner, and a blinding factor. The chain stores ciphertext.
- A spend is authorized by a **`t`-of-`n` threshold signature** produced with FROST. The key shares are never combined, so no single party can sign.
- That signature is checked **inside a zero-knowledge proof**, along with proof that the note being spent is in the pool. The chain verifies the proof and learns nothing else.
- A **relayer** submits the withdrawal and pays the fee, so the destination address has no on-chain link to the members.

The result reads like the Zcash shielded model, pointed at a Solana team treasury.
